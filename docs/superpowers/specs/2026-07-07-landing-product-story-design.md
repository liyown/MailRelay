# Landing Product Story Design

## Goal

Turn the short MailRelay homepage into a complete product story that explains why email is a powerful automation protocol, shows the golden use cases, makes ambitious but supportable claims, and converts readers into first-time users.

## Narrative

The page moves through a deliberate sales sequence:

1. **Category claim:** any device that can send email is already an automation terminal.
2. **Golden scenarios:** remote operations from a phone, constrained or legacy devices, durable asynchronous jobs, and email-to-Agent/MCP workflows.
3. **Big promise:** one inbox can become the universal remote for a single-machine automation stack—without installing another client or exposing another dashboard.
4. **Why email wins:** global availability, store-and-forward delivery, attachments, threading, and human-readable audit trails.
5. **How it works:** authenticated mail becomes a typed Command, routes through one interface, executes a declared Handler, persists state, and replies.
6. **Discovery demonstration:** `help` and `help deploy` are generated from the same command definitions.
7. **Alternative comparison:** explain where MailRelay is simpler than SSH, chat bots, raw webhooks, and custom control panels without claiming it replaces every use case.
8. **Trust proof:** sender plus token authentication, Message-ID deduplication, SQLite audit/outbox, fixed destinations, and dead-letter recovery.
9. **Handler horizon:** stable, beta, and experimental capability map.
10. **Five-minute path and final CTA.**

## Voice

- Bold: “The universal remote for everything you run.”
- Concrete: every claim is backed by an implemented behavior.
- No fabricated benchmarks, customer logos, usage counts, testimonials, or absolute security claims.
- Chinese explanatory copy with concise English category headlines, matching the current Nub-inspired visual voice.

## Visual system

- Preserve the warm paper, coral accent, Encode Sans, black terminal panels, and broad Nub-like whitespace.
- Alternate large editorial statements with paired scenario/code panels instead of a generic card grid.
- Use a comparison table, command-email mockups, Discovery responses, and a capability maturity rail as proof surfaces.
- Keep mobile sections linear, readable, and free of horizontal overflow.
